My Passions

Monta Vista High School

Link Crew Leader (August 2014 to May 2016)

My strong desire to lead by example drew me to becoming a student leader through the Link Crew program at my school. Acting as a mentor for freshmen, I eased the transition for first year students into high school by providing advice on how to be successful on a multitude of levels, including academically and socially. My goal was to be a positive role model who could provide a level of support and guidance in the nerve-wracking and overwhelming world that is high school and ultimately be a facilitator for success.

Link Crew is a year long transition program with three major components that contribute to its success: high school orientation, academic follow ups, and social follow ups. I built a mentor relationship with a group of freshmen by sharing my experiences and lessons learned throughout my first two years of high school. Alongside that, I fostered freshman academic success and character development through structured classroom visits.

Monta Vista High School

TEDx Conference 1st Place Award (May 2014)

Education has played a substantial role in my life, not only because of my various years of schooling, but also because I’ve constantly imagined different ways schools could be reinvented. One flaw I’ve perceived in education is an overemphasis on the achievement of high test scores through means of rote learning, at the cost of true understanding.

In my sophomore year of high school, I, along with three friends, gave a TED Talk to approximately 200 students, teachers, and parents about how we could reinvent education at our school by implementing a system of video-based learning where teachers’ lectures would be recorded and posted to a website. Students could pause or rewind lectures to their desire, allowing them to take agency over their learning and gain a deeper understanding of material. Our proposal was granted $1,000 to be implemented in the coming school years.

I’ve always tried to keep a quote from American biologist Leroy Hood in mind: “Don’t underestimate the power of your vision to change the world.” Ultimately, I hope to be a creator of change by utilizing my studies in computer science as a gateway to furthering the advancement of technological innovation in the educational field.
